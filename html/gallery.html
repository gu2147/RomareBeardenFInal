A BLACK ODYSSEY

Odyssey: A long wandering or voyage
usually marked by many changes of fortune.

Romare Bearden's "Odyssey" collection is a profound exploration of Homer's ancient epic, "The Odyssey," through the vibrant lens of African American culture. Completed in 1977, this series is composed of over twenty collages and watercolors, each reinterpreting pivotal moments from the ancient tale. Bearden infuses the classical narrative with elements that reflect his personal experiences and the broader history of the African diaspora.

He paints Odysseus not as a distant Greek hero but as a symbol of resilience, echoing the journey of African Americans through trials, triumphs, and the ultimate pursuit of home. The imagery is richly layered with Bearden's signature collage style, blending bold colors, abstract forms, and an intricate mix of textures. He creates a dreamlike world where the mythical and the real intertwine, inviting viewers to reconsider the ancient epic within contemporary contexts.

Battle With Cicones, 1964

"Battle with the Cicones" captures the grim consequences faced by Odysseus and his crew after sacking the city of the Cicones. Romare Bearden's collage technique, characterized by dynamic textures and contrasting colors, portrays the chaos and brutality of the Greeks’ overconfidence. Fragmented human figures are layered across the scene, creating a whirl of motion that mirrors the confusion and disarray of battle. In deep reds and cool blues, Bearden hints at both the bloodshed and the encroaching nightfall, which obscure the boundaries between victor and vanquished. This imagery conveys the bitter irony of conquest, where the Greeks’ hubris leads to the Cicones' swift reprisal. Elements of classical mythology and African heritage transforms this episode into a timeless reflection on human folly and the high cost of unchecked ambition.
