ABOUT THE ARTIST

"Art is always made from other art."
~Romare Bearden

Romare Bearden, an influential American artist, was born on September 2, 1911, in Charlotte, North Carolina, and emerged as a central figure in 20th-century art. He is renowned for his innovative collage techniques, which vividly captured the spirit, struggles, and culture of the African American experience. His work reflected not only the times in which he lived but also the breadth of African American life and identity, blending historical and contemporary influences into rich visual tapestries. Bearden's family moved to Harlem, New York, when he was a child. Growing up amid the cultural flourishing of the Harlem Renaissance, he was exposed to an environment brimming with creative energy, which profoundly impacted his artistic development. Bearden initially pursued academic studies, graduating from New York University in 1935 with a degree in education. However, his passion for art was unwavering, and he pursued painting alongside his studies.
