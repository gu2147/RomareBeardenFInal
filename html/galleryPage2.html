A BLACK ODYSSEY

Cattle of the Sun God,

Romare Bearden’s "Cattle of the Sun God" retells the myth of Odysseus’ men disobeying his orders and slaughtering the sacred cattle of Helios. The composition contrasts the sailors’ desperate hunger with the tranquil serenity of the sun god's prized herd. Bearden's layered collage technique, rich in color and texture, emphasizes the tension between greed and reverence. The brilliant golds and greens of the cattle stand out against the dark, shadowy forms of the crew, highlighting the spiritual violation that brings about their doom. By weaving Afrocentric imagery into this tale of divine retribution, Bearden enhances the universality of the story, linking the myth to broader themes of respect for sacred customs and the dangers of succumbing to human desire.

Circe,

In "Circe," Romare Bearden masterfully depicts the powerful sorceress who captivates Odysseus and his men. Circe's striking figure, rendered in Bearden’s intricate collage style, stands at the forefront of a swirling, colorful landscape that merges classical and African elements. Her commanding posture and intense gaze exude both strength and allure, emphasizing her role as an enchantress who blurs the line between seduction and danger. The layered textures and abstract symbols in the background evoke a dreamlike atmosphere, suggesting Circe's magical powers and the beguiling trap she sets for Odysseus. Bearden’s rich palette of blues, greens, and purples weaves a captivating visual spell around Circe, inviting viewers into her mystical realm where the lines between reality and illusion are artfully obscured.
